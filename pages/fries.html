Fries

Description

Perfect French fries with our easy recipe – you'll end up with lightly golden, crisp fries. They're great as a snack or side dish to a Friday night feast

Get ready for some fry-tastic fun! Today, we're whipping up a batch of golden, crispy French fries that are nothing short of perfection. We'll slice those potatoes into thin, uniform sticks, giving them a quick dip in hot oil until they're irresistibly crispy on the outside and fluffy on the inside. Sprinkle them with a pinch of salt, and you've got yourself a plate of addictive, melt-in-your-mouth fries that will vanish faster than you can say "Bon appétit!"

Ingredients

  • 1kg Odin or King Edward potatoes
  • vegetable oil, for deep frying

Steps

  1. Peel the potatoes and cut into 1cm-thick batons. Put into a bowl of cold water and leave to soak for at least 2 hrs to remove the starch from the potatoes – this will help them crisp while frying.

  2. Let's fry it up! After draining and patting dry those potatoes, heat some oil in a deep pan to around 140°C (when a bread cube turns golden in 60 seconds). Fry the fries in batches for about 6 minutes each until they're a lovely golden color, cooked through, and lightly crispy. Drain them on kitchen paper, and you're set for some delicious crispy bites!

  3. Turn the heat up until the oil reaches 180C, or a cube of bread browns in 30 seconds. Fry the French fries again for 2-3 mins per batch until lightly golden and really crisp. Drain on kitchen paper and immediately season with sea salt. Enjoy!
